The most interesting thing is that music makes abstract mathematical structure emotionally perceptible.

At one level, music is full of math: frequency ratios create intervals, rhythm divides time into patterns, harmony can be described with modular arithmetic and symmetry, and composition often uses transformations like repetition, inversion, augmentation, and recursion.

But the remarkable part is not just that music can be analyzed mathematically. It is that humans can *feel* those structures before they can name them. A simple octave is a 2:1 frequency ratio, but it is heard as sameness-at-a-higher-level. A perfect fifth is a 3:2 ratio, but it feels stable and open. Syncopation is a displacement in a temporal grid, but it feels like tension, propulsion, or surprise.

So music is a kind of embodied mathematics: number, proportion, symmetry, expectation, and transformation translated into time, sound, and emotion.
