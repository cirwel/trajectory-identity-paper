# AI-use disclosure (SAGE policy)

This manuscript was developed with substantial AI assistance in editorial and analysis roles. The disclosure below follows SAGE's AI-use policy as of May 2026.

## What AI was used for

**Drafting and revision.** Anthropic's Claude (via Claude Code CLI) was used to draft prose, restructure sections, perform symbol-naming and cross-reference consistency edits, and apply revisions identified by review passes. The author reviewed and approved every paragraph; AI-suggested changes that conflicted with the author's intent were rejected.

**Independent review (second pass).** OpenAI's Codex (gpt-5.5 model) was invoked as a second reviewer with an explicit adversarial prompt. The Codex review found three substantive issues the first-pass review had missed (a variance-prose-vs-data inconsistency, a similarity-weight double-counting bug, and an internal contradiction between the contribution claim and the scope statement). All three were fixed in the v0.11 revision. Codex's review is preserved verbatim in the public repository at `REVIEW-CODEX-2026-05-09.md`.

**Independent review (third pass).** A three-agent parallel review council (a dialectic-knowledge-architect agent, a code-reviewer agent, and a live-verifier agent) was invoked after the second pass. The council found a structural circularity in the §2.4 cardinality argument that both prior passes had missed, plus consistency-and-factual residue. All findings were addressed in the v0.12 revision. The council review is preserved at `REVIEW-COUNCIL-2026-05-09.md`.

**Empirical analysis tooling.** Reproducibility scripts in `scripts/analysis_v0.11.1.py` and `experiments/cross-llm-discrimination/` were drafted with AI assistance and exercised against real Lumen data. Numerical results in §6.4 were generated by the analysis scripts; the AI-assistance was in writing the scripts, not in producing the numbers.

## What AI was *not* used for

- Conceptual contributions and framework design.
- Decisions about which claims to make and how strongly to defend them.
- Empirical observations (the data are from a deployed embodied agent, recorded by the broker on the Pi).
- Choice of theoretical sources to engage with.
- Final editorial authority.

## How transparency is enforced

The full drafting and review trail is in the public Git history of <https://github.com/cirwel/trajectory-identity-paper>. Specifically:

- Each substantive AI-assisted revision is a separate Git commit with a descriptive message.
- The Codex and council review files (`REVIEW-CODEX-2026-05-09.md`, `REVIEW-COUNCIL-2026-05-09.md`) are committed verbatim.
- The Changelog at the end of the manuscript itself records the sequence of revisions and what each closed.

Reviewers are welcome to inspect the Git history directly to verify the role AI played in each editorial change.
